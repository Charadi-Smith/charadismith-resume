Cryptologic Language Analysis

My name is Charadi Noelle Smith. I currently major in Linguistics. I focus my research on Internationally adopted children and their adaptiveness to their adopted languages from their birth languages. I also study the morphology, phonology, phonetics, syntax, and semantics of languages like japanese, italian, and korean. Understanding these components in a language allows one to identify the structural differences between these languages and english. It is my goal to understand the linguistics of several languages in order to reach further in my research regarding internationally adopted children.

My professional experience so far is limited to my personal lab work done on campus at my university. I have studied each sub discipline in my field and I am currently training my knowledge to understand how to use it in analyzing and decoding classified cryptic codes.

My work can be used in several fields but most importantly in government environments. Analyzing and decoding cryptologic codes is meant to be used to defend businesses and prevent cyber or terrorist attacks. To prevent such attacks one must be trained and able to understand differences in languages in order to decode messages. This work is handy in government environments such as any military branch, the NSA, the CIA, the FBI, the USIC, and any intelligence agency. This work is also handy in businesses which are targets of cyber attacks such as hospitals, large corporations, or any business likely to held for ransom.
